Jayla Chee
Photo Credit: Todd Rosenberg


Jayla Chee is a New York bass player originally from Belmont, California in the Bay Area. At age 20, she is currently an undergraduate sophomore at the Juilliard School studying with Gerald Cannon. Before moving to New York, Jayla played regularly with acclaimed Bay Area Artists such as Andrew Speight and Vince Lateano. In her early career she had many opportunities to play at prestigious venues such as The Monterey Jazz Festival, the SFjazz Center, and the Black Cat. Alongside her professional engagements, she also participated in some of America’s top youth jazz programs. Some of these included Carnegie hall’s National Youth Orchestra, the NextGeneration Jazz Orchestra, and Youngarts. These programs gave Jayla the opportunity to travel and perform in Brazil, China, Taiwan, and Hong Kong. After moving to New York she quickly became engrained into the scene and has already performed with Evan Sherman and her own group as well as many other local musicians at venues like Smalls, Ginny’s and others. Jayla has shared the stage with the likes of Wynton Marsalis, Kurt Elling, and Allison Miller. On top of her major musical influences, Herbie Hancock and Elis Regina, Jayla derives immense inspiration from Nature. Her passion for natural gardening, hiking, and studying plants is very apparent in her art and personality.
